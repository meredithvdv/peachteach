What grade level would you like to teach?

Elementary School:
Elementary School lessons on PeachTeach are built to spark beginner interest in computer science through exposure to algorithms, prototyping, and building block-style code. Read about each of the modules below.

Module 1

  • Computer Basics

Module 2

  • How to engage young kids

Module 3

  • Scratch tutorials
Middle School:
Teachers hoping to improve Middle School computer science education will find lessons based around basic computer skills including typing. Other challenges include printing a basic Hello World program.

Module 1

  • Computer etiquite

Module 2

  • Keyboard skills

Module 3

  • How to safely use the internet
High School:
The high school section of PeachTeach focuses on introductory programming education. We focus on Java, the language taught in AP Computer Science and many introductory college programming classes.

Module 1

  • What is coding? Why Java?

Module 2

  • Conditional Statements

Module 3

  • Loops


What's in the future of PeachTeach?

Plans are in the works for PeachTeach to attract corporate sponsors such as Google and Apple to provide the infrastructure necessary for startup. With support from the Georgia Department of Education, we hope to provide teachers with incentives, bonuses, and career-improving credentials to really motivate them to take up this service.

Once the infrastructure is in place, we hope to recruit education professionals, experienced tech personnel, and current CS professors/teachers to build the initial curriculum using cutting-edge pedagogy research.
